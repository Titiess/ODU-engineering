## The Legacy Process

Before digitalisation, obtaining a Certificate of Origin from Owerri Municipal Council required:

- **Physical office visits** — citizens travelled to council offices, often multiple times
- **Manual paperwork** — handwritten forms processed by administrative staff
- **Slow approvals** — processing took days to weeks depending on administrative backlog
- **Opaque tracking** — citizens had no visibility into their application status
- **Fraud vulnerability** — paper certificates were easily forged with no verification mechanism
- **Poor accessibility** — citizens in remote areas faced significant barriers to access

## Why This Mattered

Certificate of Origin documents are critical for Nigerian citizens. They are required for:

- Employment applications
- Educational admissions
- Property transactions
- Government contracts
- Identity verification

The inefficiency of the manual process created real friction in citizens' lives and undermined trust in local government services.

## The Opportunity

Digitising this workflow was not simply a technology project — it was an opportunity to fundamentally improve how citizens interact with their local government. The platform needed to be accessible, trustworthy, and efficient enough to make the digital alternative clearly superior to the in-person experience.
